When a Sprint is useful

One defined result.

  • A workflow causes repeated delays or rework.
  • A handover or responsibility needs clarifying.
  • Important information is difficult to see.
  • A manual task could be simplified.
  • A Review priority is ready to be implemented.

We must be able to define the result and boundaries before beginning.

What a Sprint may involve

Practical change, not a programme.

  • Simplifying a workflow.
  • Clarifying ownership and handovers.
  • Improving a form, template or operating guide.
  • Connecting information between existing tools.
  • Reducing repeated entry, checking or follow-up.
  • Introducing a proportionate tool where it earns its place.

What you receive

A change the team can actually use.
